Katrina Cullen

Hi, I'm Katrina, a second year PhD student in the School of Earth and Environmental Sciences at the University of Manchester. I belong to the Organic Geochemistry research group. My research is about an adsorbent call NyexTM used to treat water pollution. I basically get paid to ask lots of the 'stupid questions' about NyexTM for four years, and call myself Dr. at the end!

Well, what is NyexTM?

Good question! NyexTM is a graphite based adsorbent created by Arvia Technology Ltd. To put it simply, it acts like a sponge and adsorbs organics out of water. You can then pass electricity through a bed of NyexTM to blast the organics apart and destroy them. The Nyex is then free to soak up more organics and the cycle starts again!
